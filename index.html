App Inventor: MegaTube

Duration: September 19, 2017 - October 5th, 2017
Objective: To create an app to view videos on YouTube
Group: Safwan Kasim
Course: Computer Science and Engineering (CSE)
Rubric
The purpose of the app is to have multiple resizable video players that can be in full screen. In order to make our app different from the base app, we will be adding a list function, where you can select videos from a list, rather than just being able to play a limited amount of videos on the main screen.
  1. We used a 3rd party server to extract the stream url to set as the source for the video player. We did this as appinventor cannot play videos through the web-view blocks.
  2. We used a simple if-else statement to decide to enlarge or shrink the display.
  3. The full screen buttons are powered with event listeners to make the video go fullscreen
In making this app, we had ambitious goals in beginning. Over time we realized App Inventors Limits. We wanted to play Youtube videos through the web-view component, but App inventor does not support videos on the web, only videos on the video player. So we came up with another solution, connect the app to a pre-existing API to fetch youtube videos and their sources. We came up with a solution that can be used in many different contexts and makes up for the shortcomings of the web-view. Although our solution is far from perfect, it is usable in the context of the videowall. We switched at the last minute to a drop down list as a lot of search terms can not be parsed to be URL safe, thus we gave the user a list options to choose from. Another problem that we could not fix was the resizing issue, the button would not work properly even though the code is identical to its tutorial counterpart. There is a lot of things we could do better but we did not have the time to, we focused more on getting App inventor to pull videos from youtube, the core concept of our app. As we developed further in our app, we realized that we were starting to the solve the problem in the world that we set out with on this journey, to be able to watch youtube videos at once on the same screen. Although we did not fully solve as the user cannot search to his/her’s content, we did manage to get a select few videos to work. In the end we did get something that works and works well besides the few inevitable errors that make into the final product. My main responsibility was to program the solution as well as to document our solution.

Work Experience


Picture from aboard the Golden Bear traning ship at California State University Maritime Academy
Since I began High School my experience with engineering grew and most of them are thanks to Dublin High School's engineering program. As a student of the Dublin High's Engineering Academy, I am able to attend various field trip and listen to a wide range of guest speakers from throughout the industry. One of the most memorable field trips was the one to California State University Maritime Academy (Cal Maritime). I was surprised to find out that this was the only maritime academy on the west coast. At Cal Maritime, I was given a tour of the campus and an idea of how daily life at the campus is, and it opened my eyes to a different view on how college can be. Cal Maritime takes a more hands on approach on learning, something that differentiates it from other colleges. On this field trip I realized that prestigious colleges may not provide everything needed to succeed in life, Cal Maritime provides hands on experience as well help on all different aspects of an engineering jobs. One thing I found interesting was that they even give lessons on etiquette as well. Cal Maritime also made mechanical engineering much more appealing, I’ve always thought that it was set in stone that I would go into computer science as a major, but now after the field trip I consider both mechanical engineering and computer science equally as valid options as a major.

I also went on other field trips to places such as the Autodesk Headquarters. I saw how other people have been using Autodesk software as a way to design, prototype, and communicate their ideas. I also went on a virtual field trip to the Livermore Airport where I learned about how the airport operates and everything that goes into having a successful take off and landing.

Throughout my time in the Engineering Academy, I’ve had the opportunity to listen to various different guest speakers. The most interesting guest speaker in my opinion was Maher Salloum. He had a profound impact on my view of how the things you learn in school can be transferred to real life problems. In his presentation he went over two main things: data compression and cancer research. His explanation on data compression showed how he and his team came up with a solution by developing an algorithm to compress data with little loss of quality. His next topic was using magnets to heat up metal to remove cancer cells. His team used a combination of chemistry and biology to tackle a problem that takes the lives of millions each year. His talk, specifically his cancer research that he described, sparked an interest in biotechnology for me.

Another guest speaker that I enjoyed listening to was Jason Yosinski who works as a software engineer at Uber. He had an in depth presentation about machine learning and how machine learning algorithms interpret and process data. He showed many demonstrations showing how computers visualize and read images coming in from a camera. He also talked about the real life applications of image recognitions and other machine learning algorithms and how they can be used to solve real world problems.

At the Engineering Academy, I was also able to join its mentorship program where I met Paul O'Flynn. Paul works as a software engineer at Workday and was able to give me an insight on how it is to work as a software developer at a large scale company. He also provided invaluable advice on how to choose which college to apply to and how to handle difficult interview questions. Overall, I had a very positive experience in the mentorship program and I am grateful to all the teachers and volunteers who made it happen.
